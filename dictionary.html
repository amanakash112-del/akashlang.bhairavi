How to Read a Dictionary Card

Card Structure Each card shows: Root (the base concept) · Part-of-speech tag · English meaning · Domain
Below: Noun form (-ombu) · Verb dictionary form (-uvu) · Adjective form (-oku)
Then: a usage example sentence.

Understanding Class-Neutral Roots

Every root in Joylang is class-neutral — it carries meaning but no inherent word class. The class is assigned by the suffix:

SuffixClassExample (root: keshi = hair)
-ombuNounkeshombu = the hair, a hair
-uvuVerb (citation)keshuvu = to groom / to style hair
-okuAdjectivekeshoku = hair-like / hairy

Colour Verbs — Actually Valid

Colour roots used as verbs mean "to make something become that colour". These are intentional and useful:

Joylang VerbMeaningExample Use
boruvuto brown / to make brownAnu kashobu-ko boruvu-lo. (I am browning the bread.)
kiruvuto yellow / to make yellowParna-chi kiruvu-me. (The leaves have yellowed.)
veluvuto green / to make greenBarisha-chi gardiombu veluvu-me. (The rain has greened the garden.)
lakuvuto blacken / to make blackDhuanu-chi akashu lakuvu-me. (The smoke has blackened the sky.)
laluvuto redden / to make redSurya vosilo akashu laluvu-lo. (The sunset reddens the sky.)
